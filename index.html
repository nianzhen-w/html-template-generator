Image Size Quick Reference

🖥 PC / Wide Screenshots

Use max-width: 300px or 350px

These are landscape/horizontal images from desktop screens.

📱 Mobile / Tall Screenshots

Use max-width: 200px

These are portrait/vertical images from phone screens (iPhone, etc.)

📐 Side-by-Side Layout

Only the first image gets wrapped in <span style="margin-right: 20px">

The second image is just a plain <img> tag.

<span style="margin-right: 20px"><img src="LEFT.jpg" style="max-width: 300px;"></span> <img src="RIGHT.jpg" style="max-width: 300px;">

🎯 Single Image (with span wrapper)

All single images also use span wrapper for consistency:

<span style="margin-right: 20px"><img src="IMAGE.jpg" style="max-width: 300px;"></span>

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What's the difference between section and div?

section = a meaningful chapter with a heading (like "Chapter 1").
div = a generic box to group things together (no special meaning).
Visually they look the same. Section is for big topics, div is for sub-blocks within.

Q: Can I use the same block multiple times?

Yes! Just drag it from the left multiple times. Each drag creates a new copy in your template.

Q: What do the【brackets】mean in the output?

They're placeholders! Replace them with your actual content (title, URLs, descriptions, etc.)

Q: Why does every image have a span wrapper?

For consistency. The span + margin-right ensures spacing works uniformly whether you have 1 image or 2 side-by-side.

Q: Do I need to add alt="" to images?

Our current setup doesn't require alt attributes. But if accessibility is needed later, add alt="description" to each img tag.

Q: What's a TBU placeholder?

"To Be Updated" — it's a commented-out section that won't show on the page. Use it to reserve space for future content.
